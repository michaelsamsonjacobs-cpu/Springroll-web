# Contract Finder Workflow

Systematically find, evaluate, and pursue federal and state contract opportunities.

## Primary Data Sources

| Source | URL | Coverage |
|--------|-----|----------|
| **SAM.gov** | [sam.gov](https://sam.gov) | All federal contracts |
| **GovWin** | [govwin.com](https://www.govwin.com) | Intelligence + forecasting |
| **FPDS** | [fpds.gov](https://www.fpds.gov) | Historical contract data |
| **USAspending** | [usaspending.gov](https://www.usaspending.gov) | Spending transparency |
| **Agency Portals** | Various | Agency-specific opportunities |

---

## Step 1: Register in SAM.gov

Before pursuing contracts, complete registration:

- [ ] Get UEI (Unique Entity Identifier)
- [ ] Complete SAM.gov entity registration
- [ ] Set up annual renewal reminders
- [ ] Add relevant NAICS codes
- [ ] Update capabilities statement

> [!IMPORTANT]
> SAM registration takes 7-10 business days. Start early!

---

## Step 2: Search SAM.gov Contract Opportunities

### Navigate to Search
1. Go to [sam.gov/search](https://sam.gov/search/?index=opp)
2. Select "Contract Opportunities"

### Filter Options
| Filter | Options |
|--------|---------|
| **Type** | Presolicitation, Sources Sought, Combined Synopsis, Solicitation |
| **Set-Aside** | Small Business, 8(a), HUBZone, WOSB, SDVOSB |
| **NAICS** | Your industry codes |
| **Place of Performance** | State/Region |
| **Posted Date** | Last 7/30/90 days |

### Keyword Strategies
```
Good:
- "artificial intelligence" AND "defense"
- "autonomous systems" OR "unmanned"
- NAICS:541715

Avoid:
- Single generic words
- Overly narrow terms
```

## Step 3: Understand Opportunity Types

| Type | What It Means | Action |
|------|---------------|--------|
| **Sources Sought** | Agency exploring options | Respond with capabilities |
| **RFI** | Request for Information | Provide detailed response |
| **Presolicitation** | Coming soon | Prepare, watch for RFP |
| **Combined Synopsis** | Solicitation included | Evaluate and bid |
| **Solicitation** | Formal RFP/RFQ | Submit proposal |

## Step 4: Research Before Bidding

### Incumbent Analysis
```markdown
## Incumbent Research Checklist
- [ ] Current contract holder (check FPDS)
- [ ] Contract value and performance period
- [ ] Recompete or new requirement?
- [ ] Set-aside status changes?
- [ ] Pain points with incumbent?
```

### Agency Research
- Past awards in this area (USAspending)
- Contracting officer contacts
- Small business goals and targets
- Preferred contract vehicles

---

## Step 5: Evaluate Bid/No-Bid

Score opportunities (1-10):

| Criteria | Weight | Questions |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| **Technical Fit** | 25% | Can we do this work? |
| **Past Performance** | 20% | Have we done similar? |
| **Competitive Position** | 20% | Can we win? |
| **Profitability** | 15% | Worth the effort? |
| **Strategic Value** | 10% | Opens new doors? |
| **Resources** | 10% | Do we have capacity? |

### Bid/No-Bid Decision Tree
```
Score > 70 → BID
Score 50-70 → TEAM (find prime/sub partner)
Score < 50 → NO BID
```

## Step 7: Proposal Essentials

### Standard Proposal Sections
1. **Technical Approach** — How you'll do the work
2. **Management Approach** — Team, org chart, processes
3. **Past Performance** — 3-5 relevant references
4. **Price/Cost Volume** — Detailed pricing

### Compliance Matrix
| Requirement | Section | Page | Compliant? |
|-------------|---------|------|------------|
| L.1.1 Technical Approach | 2.0 | 5 | ✓ |
| L.1.2 Key Personnel | 2.3 | 12 | ✓ |

## Contract Vehicles

### GSA Schedules
If you do repeated government work, consider GSA Schedule:
- Streamlined procurement for buyers
- Pre-negotiated pricing
- 5-year base + options

## Set-Aside Categories

| Set-Aside | Requirement | Benefit |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Small Business** | Meet SBA size standards | Reduced competition |
| **8(a)** | Socially disadvantaged | Sole source up to $4M |
| **HUBZone** | Located in HUBZone | Price preference |
| **WOSB** | Woman-owned | Industry restrictions |
| **SDVOSB** | Veteran-owned | VA priority |

## Teaming Strategies

### When to Team
- Lack past performance in this area
- Missing required certifications
- Need set-aside status you don't have
- Contract too large to handle alone

### Finding Partners
1. SAM.gov entity search by NAICS/capability
2. GovWin teaming network
3. Small Business Administration matchmaking
4. Industry days and conferences

### Teaming Agreement Essentials
- [ ] Roles and responsibilities
- [ ] Work share percentages
- [ ] Pricing approach
- [ ] Exclusivity terms
- [ ] NDA provisions

## Common Mistakes

1. **Bidding everything** — Focus on winnable opportunities
2. **Ignoring incumbents** — Research who holds current contract
3. **Missing compliance** — Follow instructions exactly
4. **Weak pricing** — Don't underbid to the point of loss
5. **Late submissions** — Submit 24+ hours early
